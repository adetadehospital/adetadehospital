COVID-19 Policy

Following the Nigeria Centre for Disease Control (NCDC) advisory, at Ade-Tade hospital we have a “no mask, no entry” policy. To protect you and to protect ourselves, all members of our team wear face masks. We respectfully expect you to do the same. Please wear a cloth mask or medical mask whether you are a patient, a family member, or a visitor, for as long as you are on our premises. If we need to examine your face or a procedure requires access to your face, we will direct you on how and when to remove your mask.

To reduce the risk of infection or spread of COVID-19, NCDC advises members of the public to adhere to the following hand and respiratory hygiene measures:

  • Wash your hands regularly with soap and water or use an alcohol-based sanitiser if no water and soap is available.
  • Use a non-medical face mask for all persons in public spaces.
  • Practice no-touch greetings.
  • Maintain at least 2 metres physical distance between yourself and anyone who is coughing or sneezing.
  • Avoid crowded spaces such as open markets, crowded supermarkets, and pharmacies.
  • Adhere to other national and state directives.

Registration

Upon your arrival at our hospital, you will be greeted by a nurse and asked for your information and the reason for your visit. If you already have a file with us, you may be required to provide your small card or your patient number so that we can pull up your file. If you are visiting our hospital for the first time, a new file will be opened for you and you will be given a mini-card with your patient number on it. Bringing your mini-card with you each time you visit will make it faster and easier for us to pull up your record.
