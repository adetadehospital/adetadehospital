Repair of a Low Anal Fistula
—a painful, often embarrassing condition.


Author: Dr. Adeyemo Tade, MBChB, DMP, FAGP





An elderly man, a little over sixty years of age, presented primarily with a chronic history of discharge from his perineal area. He had tried several remedies for about six weeks prior to presentation. A thorough history and physical examination, particularly of his perineum, as well as digital rectal examination helped to rule out other conditions and narrow down the working diagnosis to “Fistula-in-ano”. The most obvious finding was the presence of a discharging sinus about 15cm inferior-lateral to his anal orifice. There was no swelling around the sinus.

Impression: Fistula-in-ano

Jump to the procedure

A fistula-in-ano (or anal fistula) is an abnormal tract that connects an opening inside the anal canal to an opening in the perianal skin. There may be a foul-smelling discharge and pain. Both symptoms are potential sources of discomfort and stigma. Surgeons around the world agree that the treatment of fistula-in-ano still remains very challenging (Poggio, 2022). However, the treatment of choice is surgery, the goals of which are to : (a) Drain any abscess and (b) Remove the fistula (tract) while taking measures to preserve anal sphincter function and avoid recurrence of the fistula.
There are two varieties of Fistula-in-ano, High and Low, depending on whether the opening into the ano-rectal canal is above or below the Levator Ani muscle. Some classifications also use the part of the anal sphincter involved — upper two-third of anal sphincter (high fistula) or lower third of anal sphincter (low fistula). Differentiation of these two types can be by a simple dye test. Two pieces of gauze are placed in the ano-rectal canal, one above and one below the sphincter. Gentian Violet ((GV) is then injected through the external opening of the fistula (Farquharson et al., 2005). The stained piece of gauze indicates the location of the internal opening.


Since the low variety of anal fistula is the preponderant type, I always prepare any patient who has anal fistula for a fistulotomy (a surgical procedure to treat low fistulas) which will be done after first confirming the type of fistula by examination under anesthesia during the same surgery. This saves the patient the hassles of two separate procedures. I however let the patient know of the small possibility that the fistula might turn out to be the high type which would necessitate aborting the procedure and making a referral to a specialist instead.


Fistulotomy involves laying open the fistula tract


The Procedure

During this patient's surgical procedure (which I usually do under general anesthesia to minimize cost for the patient, and because the alternative - spinal anesthesia, will cause loss of sphincter tone and reduced ability to visualize the length of the anal sphincter), I introduced a thin probe through the external opening of the sinus and pushed it towards the anal canal where my finger therein could readily feel the probe and the internal opening of the fistula in the anal canal. I then brought the end of the probe in the anal canal out through the anal orifice.



Thereafter, I cut out all the tissue between the anus and the external opening of the fistula, practically excising the fistula track. *(Method from Farquharson's textbook of operative surgery).



The wound was packed for 48 hours., after which daily sitz bath and dry dressing were done. The patient also completed a course of antibiotics (Ceftriaxone, Doxycycline, and Flagyl).



The wound healed very well, and the patient was very happy.
